Contact us

while holding a letter *

The TML relies on the collaborations of scholars, publishers, and libraries to improve and expand the project for the benefit of scholarship and education. If you like to contribute to the TML, or if you have concerns, suggestions, or general question about its content and use, please write or email us at:

Thesaurus Musicarum Latinarum
Center for the History of Music Theory and Literature
Indiana University Jacobs School of Music
1201 East Third Street
Bloomington, IN 47405
chmtl@indiana.edu.

The Center for the History of Music Theory and Literature maintains a mailing list and a Twitter account where news about the projects managed by the Center, including the publication of new TML texts, are posted. Instructions on how to subscribe are found on the CHMTL website.

We are deeply grateful to all those who made donations to the CHMTL Endowment Fund, established in 2012, which is also intended to help towards the continuation of this project.

* Julius Caesar swims to a boat while holding a letter. Boccaccio, Des cas des nobles hommes et femmes. Los Angeles, The J. Paul Getty Museum, MS. 63 (France 1413-15), fol. 198v. Courtesy of the Getty's Open Content Program.
